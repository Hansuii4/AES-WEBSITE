Basic Nondestructive Testing

A Basic Nondestructive Testing (NDT) course covering Ultrasonic Testing (UT), Radiographic Testing (RT), Magnetic Particle Testing (MT), and Liquid Penetrant Testing (PT). This course provides fundamental knowledge and practical skills for inspecting materials without causing damage.

UT uses high-frequency sound waves to detect internal flaws, while RT employs X-rays or gamma rays to create images of internal structures. MT is effective for identifying surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic materials, and PT involves applying a liquid dye to reveal surface cracks.

Industry regulations such as ASNT, ASTM, and ISO provide guidelines for testing procedures and certification requirements. Practical training allows students to apply these techniques, analyze test results, and document findings accurately.

No. of Training Hours: 48 hrs
No. of Days: 6 days

Magnetic Particle Testing & Liquid Penetrant Testing (Level 1 & 2)

A Magnetic Particle Testing (MT) and Liquid Penetrant Testing (PT) Level 1 & Level 2 course provides comprehensive training in these widely used nondestructive testing (NDT) methods for detecting surface and near-surface defects in materials.

At Level 1, trainees learn the basic principles, equipment handling, and step-by-step inspection procedures for both techniques. MT involves applying a magnetic field and iron particles to identify flaws in ferromagnetic materials, while PT uses liquid dyes to highlight surface defects in both metallic and non-metallic materials. This level focuses on understanding test techniques, equipment operation, safety procedures, and identifying indications under supervision.

At Level 2, trainees advance their knowledge to independently conduct and interpret tests, evaluate indications, and prepare detailed reports. They gain a deeper understanding of defect characterization, process control, and industry standards such as ASNT, ASTM, and ISO.

For MT, Level 2 training covers techniques like dry and wet methods, continuous and residual magnetization, and proper demagnetization procedures. In PT, trainees learn about different penetrant types, developer applications, and methods like water-washable and post-emulsifiable penetrants.

With hands-on practice and theoretical knowledge, certified Level 2 inspectors can supervise Level 1 personnel, assess test reliability, and make critical decisions regarding component integrity in industries like aerospace, manufacturing, and construction.

No. of Training Hours: 32 hrs (Each level)
No. of Days: 4 days (Each level)

Ultrasonic Testing (UT) Level 1 & Level 2

A Ultrasonic Testing (UT) Level 1 & Level 2 course provides essential training for inspecting materials using high-frequency sound waves to detect internal defects.

At Level 1, trainees learn the fundamental principles of sound wave propagation, transducer operation, and basic flaw detection techniques. They become familiar with ultrasonic equipment, calibration procedures, couplants, and straight and angle beam testing methods. Level 1 personnel are trained to follow established procedures, perform inspections under supervision, and identify basic flaw indications, ensuring structural integrity in industries such as aerospace, construction, and manufacturing.

At Level 2, trainees advance their expertise to independently set up and calibrate UT equipment, interpret test results, and evaluate indications for acceptability. This level includes in-depth training on defect characterization, scanning techniques, and industry standards such as ASNT, ASTM, and ISO.

Level 2 technicians also learn about advanced techniques like Phased Array Ultrasonic Testing (PAUT) and Time-of-Flight Diffraction (TOFD). With practical training and theoretical knowledge, Level 2 inspectors can supervise Level 1 personnel, develop testing procedures, and make critical decisions regarding defect acceptability, playing a crucial role in ensuring material quality and safety.

No. of Training Hours: 40 hrs (Each level)
No. of Days: 5 days (Each level)

Radiographic Testing (RT) Level 1 & Level 2

A Radiographic Testing (RT) Level 1 & Level 2 course provides specialized training in using X-rays and gamma rays to inspect internal structures of materials for hidden defects.

At Level 1, trainees learn the fundamental principles of radiation physics, radiographic film processing, and equipment operation. They become familiar with radiation safety protocols, exposure techniques, image quality indicators, and defect recognition. Level 1 personnel are trained to follow written procedures, set up radiographic tests, process film or digital images, and identify basic discontinuities under the supervision of a certified Level 2 inspector.

At Level 2, trainees advance their skills to independently conduct and interpret radiographic tests, evaluate image quality, and assess defects based on industry standards like ASNT, ASTM, and ISO. They gain expertise in film and digital radiography, exposure calculations, scatter radiation control, and advanced image interpretation.

Level 2 personnel can develop testing procedures, make accept/reject decisions, supervise Level 1 technicians, and ensure compliance with safety regulations. Their expertise is critical in industries such as aerospace, oil & gas, and manufacturing, where detecting internal flaws in welds, castings, and pipelines ensures structural integrity and operational safety.

No. of Training Hours: 40 hrs (Each level)
No. of Days: 5 days (Each level)

Radiographic Testing (RT) Film Interpretation Level 1 & Level 2

A Radiographic Testing (RT) Film Interpretation Level 1 & Level 2 course focuses on developing the skills needed to analyze and interpret radiographic images for detecting internal defects in materials.

At Level 1, trainees learn the fundamentals of radiographic principles, image formation, and defect identification. They gain knowledge of film processing, exposure techniques, contrast and density evaluation, and the effects of scatter radiation. Level 1 personnel are trained to recognize basic discontinuities, such as porosity, cracks, and inclusions, while following established procedures and working under the supervision of a certified Level 2 inspector.

At Level 2, trainees build on their foundational knowledge to independently assess radiographic films, interpret complex indications, and make accept/reject decisions based on industry standards such as ASNT, ASTM, and ISO. This level covers advanced image analysis techniques, sensitivity evaluation, and defect characterization in welds, castings, and forgings.

Level 2 personnel are responsible for verifying film quality, ensuring compliance with radiographic acceptance criteria, and supervising Level 1 technicians. Their expertise is critical in industries like aerospace, oil & gas, and construction, where accurate interpretation of radiographic images ensures the safety and reliability of critical components.

No. of Training Hours: 40 hrs
No. of Days: 5 days
